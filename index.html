01

Privacy policy

Google requires a privacy policy URL in your store listing so users can see how you handle personal and sensitive data.

  • Host a policy that matches what your app actually does (collection, use, sharing, retention).
  • Keep the URL working and update the policy when practices change.
  • Align the policy with Data safety and any SDKs or analytics you use.

02

Ads

You must declare whether your app contains ads. Play shows a Contains ads label when you say yes.

  • Declare accurately — includes third-party ad SDKs, house ads, and promotional content shown like ads.
  • Update the declaration if you add or remove advertising.

Ads declaration Ads & monetization policies

03

App access

If reviewers cannot use all features without credentials, membership, location, or special hardware, you must provide instructions to access restricted parts.

  • Include test accounts, demo logins, or step-by-step paths that work for Google’s review team.
  • Explain paywalls, region locks, or IP restrictions if they affect review.

App access & restrictions

04

Content ratings

Complete the rating questionnaire so official authorities (e.g. IARC) assign age-based ratings shown on your store listing.

  • Answer honestly about violence, language, user interaction, and other questionnaire topics.
  • Revisit ratings when you add features or content that could change classification.

Content ratings

05

Target audience & content

Declare the age groups your app targets. Apps appealing to children face stricter requirements (Families policies, ads, data practices).

  • Declare all target age groups that reflect your design and marketing, not only who you prefer.
  • If children are in scope, plan for Families program rules and restricted data handling.

Target audience & content Designed for Families

06

Data safety

Disclose collection, sharing, security practices, and whether data is optional or required. This appears on your store listing.

  • Inventory data types: account info, location, financial info, health, identifiers, app activity, etc.
  • Declare sharing with third parties (including SDKs) and whether data is encrypted in transit.
  • Users can request deletion if you declare deletion support — honor your commitments.

Data safety form Data practices (Android docs)

07

Advertising ID

Declare whether your app uses the advertising ID (including via SDKs). Required for releases targeting newer Android versions.

  • If you use ads or measurement that reads GAID, declare usage and respect opt-out / limitations policies.
  • If you do not use it, declare that — but verify no dependency pulls it in unnoticed.

Advertising ID

08

Government apps

State whether the app is built for or used by a government entity (national, regional, local). Google routes these for appropriate review.

  • Applies to official government services, not every app that mentions civic topics.
  • Keep this accurate when ownership or purpose changes.

Government apps & requirements

09

Financial features

Declare banking, loans, trading, crypto, BNPL, and similar features so Google can apply the right policy and regional rules.

  • List all financial product types your app offers or facilitates, including via embedded WebViews or partners.
  • Expect additional verification or licensing expectations in some regions.

10

Health apps

Declare health-related features (e.g. medical, fitness, mental health, symptoms, records) so Google can enforce Health apps policy requirements.

  • Covers diagnosis/treatment claims, regulated devices, handling of health data, and certain wellness scopes.
  • Medical or clinical claims often need strong evidence and compliance with local regulations.
